About Me

Hello! I'm Kavya, and I'm passionate about technology and its intersection with biology. I am currently pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science with a minor in Applied Biosciences at Arizona State University, where I also hold a Certificate in Symbolic, Linguistic, and Cognitive Systems. My technical skills include Java, C, C++, HTML, MATLAB, Bash, Linux, SQL, JavaFX, Arduino, Assembly, and Microsoft 365.

I was a Research Scholar in the Biocomputing Scholars Program since January 2022. I worked on a project called PREP, which enhanced automated software repair in C to improve performance across multiple repair tools and datasets.

In terms of projects, I've developed a personal Valentines website that prompts users to accept a Valentine request with a dynamic "no" button that moves and displays cat images until "yes" is selected. For my CSE 240 Final Project, I created an algorithm to find the shortest path between nodes in a graph using Dijkstra's algorithm. In my CSE 120 Capstone project, I designed a custom circuit simulating an island scenario with residents and visitors crossing a bridge under set constraints, and I built an autonomous miniature car programmed to navigate a maze for my FSE 100 project.

In addition to my academic pursuits, I am actively involved in extracurricular activities. As a Scholar in the Grand Challenges Scholars Program, I engage in research, interdisciplinary coursework, service events, and entrepreneurship. I am also an active member of Theta Tau, a professional engineering fraternity, where I serve as a New Member Educator and contribute to the Professionalism Committee. Additionally, I participate in Combat Robotics, building a combat robot for semester showcases, and I am a member of NeuroDevils, where I learn about research in neuroscience and cognitive science.

My Goals

My primary goal is to pursue a career in biocomputing or computational neuroscience. I am deeply interested in how technology can enhance our understanding of biological systems and improve healthcare solutions. I aim to leverage my skills in computer science to create innovative tools and applications that can assist in diagnosing and treating diseases, ultimately contributing to advancements in personalized medicine.

In the short term, I plan to engage in internships and research opportunities that will allow me to apply my technical skills in real-world scenarios.

Long-term, I aspire to pursue a graduate degree in computational neuroscience, where I can further my knowledge and research in neural networks and artificial intelligence.
